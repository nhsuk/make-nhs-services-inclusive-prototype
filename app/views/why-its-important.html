Why inclusive design is important

NHS services are for everyone

Point 5 in the NHS service standard is about making sure that everyone can use our services.

That means building inclusive services so that everyone gets the care they need.

The aim of inclusive design is to make sure everyone can use healthcare services to reach their full potential for health and wellbeing. People should be able to do this regardless of:

  • the conditions in which they are born, grow, live, work, play and age
  • political, legal and economic factors
  • collectively shared beliefs and expectations
  • instituitional processes
  • any temporary or permanent issue which affects a person's capability or capacity
  • systemic disadvantage

Inclusivity is a key founding principle of the NHS

Inclusive design supports the NHS Constitution, which says:

  • the NHS belongs to the people
  • we provide a comprehensive service available to all based on clinical need
  • people come into contact with us at times of basic human need when care and compassion are what matter most
  • we maximise our response for the benefit of the whole community and make sure nobody is excluded, discriminated against or left behind from birth to older age

Inclusive services benefit everyone

Inclusive design can lead to the following outcomes.

  • More people can do what they need to when accessing NHS services because the services meet their needs.
  • NHS services are accessible so they can be used by everyone, including people who use assistive technology. Asscessibility is one of the many important elements of inclusive design.
  • We do not use public money to fix services that do not meet people's needs.
  • More people have the choice of when and where to access healthcare services, for example through digital channels. This can free up frontline staff to spend more time with those most in need of human care.
  • Teams feel empowered to design better services as they have the time, space and organisational backing to learn from the people they are designing with.
  • Diverse teams feel supported and able to do their best work.
