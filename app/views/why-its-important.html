Why it's important

Contributors

NHS services are for everyone

Point 5 in the NHS service standard is about making sure that everyone can use our services.

That means building inclusive services so that everyone gets the care they need.

Examples

Some users face problems when they use NHS services because of:

  • their socio-economic status or living in poverty
  • “protected characteristics” like ethnicity, gender reassignment, sexual orientation or disability (see the protected characteristics on the Equalities and Human Rights Commission website)
  • being part of a vulnerable group, such as Travellers, rough sleepers or sex workers
  • where they live

Inclusive, accessible services are better for everyone.

What we mean by inclusion and accessibility

This is what the guidance on the NHS service standard, point 5, says.

Inclusion is about responding to people's differences so that everyone:

  • feels NHS services are for them
  • can access the services they need, regardless of their background, identity or circumstances

Accessibility is about making digital services work for everyone, including people who face barriers related to:

  • hearing, like people who are Deaf or have hearing loss
  • mobility, like people who find it difficult to use a mouse
  • thinking or understanding in a different way, like autistic people or people with dyslexia or learning difficulties
  • vision, like people who are blind, partially sighted or colour blind
