What we mean by inclusive design

This definition of inclusive design is for anyone who designs, delivers, or is responsible for an NHS England product or service.

Inclusive design is an approach to designing and delivering NHS services so that everyone can access them regardless of their background or life experience.

Everyone who leads, designs or delivers an NHS service has a role in making services inclusive.

This involves recognising where people might experience barriers when accessing services and promoting an inclusive workplace culture, mindset and processes.

Point 5 of the NHS service standard is about making sure everyone can use a service. This includes more information about what we mean by inclusion and accessibility.
