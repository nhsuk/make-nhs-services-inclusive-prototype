When you introduce yourself, tell the person your pronouns

This normalises asking for pronouns, and allows everyone to say how they would like to be addressed.

Never assume someone's pronouns.

Example

Hi my name is Jude and my pronouns are She/Her

If you are showing non-inclusive content, give people trigger warnings at the start of the research

At the beginning of the interview explain that there may be content that they come across that may be triggering, and why it might be triggering.

This is important because:

  • people may need to mentally prepare themselves
  • people may not feel able to deal with the content during the session and may have to stop, in which case it's important that you support them to do this

To avoid priming the participant you should try to not use the word trigger.

Example

"We're going to show you a pages on breastfeeding. Some of these pages are old, and we know that both gender and pregnancy can be difficult subjects to talk about. If at any point you need to leave or stop, please do so."
