Research with people with physical or motor disabilities

Contributors

Adapt co-design activities

Activities that involve fine motor skills, like cutting out text or sketching, can be difficult for some users. Make sure there are alternative activities for them to do.

Gather data by observing participants

If participants have a severe disability and need to use alternative ways to communicate, for example eye-tracking technology, it can help to collect observational data. To do this, create a list of tasks and indicators that match your hypothesis and observe if the user successfully completes the tasks.

Ask closed questions

Usually user researchers ask lots of open questions. It's easier for participants who are non-verbal and rely on a communication device, if you design your research to include more closed questions.
