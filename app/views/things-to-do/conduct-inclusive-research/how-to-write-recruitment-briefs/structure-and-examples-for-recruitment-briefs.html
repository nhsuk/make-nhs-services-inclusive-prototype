Structure and examples for recruitment briefs

Structure

Here is a template you can use for a range of situations.

Hi,

We're planning to conduct [insert number and length] research sessions via [for example, Teams or Zoom] on [insert date or week].

We are working on [this service or this team] and we need to hear from people who [explain the key thing you're looking for, for example, have booked GP appointments digitally].

An example of what we are looking for:

  • age range
  • location or residency [for example, do they have to live in England?]
  • gender mix [explain the mix, and how important this is]
  • work status or job role [for example, not working, GP administrator]
  • any habits or specifications that are relevant to the research [for example, they are NHS App users, they are carers for their parents]
  • any experience with NHS services [does anything exclude them from the research?]
  • technological literacy [comfortable using the internet or not comfortable at all
  • access needs

Exclusions

List everything that means that they cannot participate in the research. For example:

  • no health workers
  • no previous experience of NHS research

Additional or nice to have requests

  • It would be great if you could find some [x], but this is not essential.

Please let me know if this is something you can help with, and how much you think it will cost.

Thank you

Examples

Here are examples to illustrate how to use the structure above.

Example

3 participants who have never been invited to breast cancer screening or attended a mammogram:

  • aged 40 to 52
  • mix of socio-economic groups
  • mix of work status (full time, part time, not working)
  • at least 1 participant whose first language is not English
  • at least 1 participant who uses a wheelchair or has difficulty standing for long periods of time and uses walking aids (such as a stick)
  • at least 1 participant whose mother or sister has had breast cancer

3 participants who have attended breast cancer screening at least once:

  • aged 50 to 65
  • mix of socio-economic groups
  • mix of work status (full time, part time, not working)
  • at least 1 participant whose first language is not English
  • at least 1 participant who uses a wheelchair or has difficulty standing for long periods of time and uses walking aids (such as a stick)

Exclusions

  • None of the participants should be cis-male.
  • None of the participants should have had breast cancer in the past or be undergoing treatment for breast cancer.
  • None of the participants should work as healthcare professionals, but people working in social care or as careworkers can be included.
  • None of the participants should work in IT, marketing, media, design or communications roles.
  • No more than 3 participants should be White British.

Additional request

  • If possible, it would be great to have 1 participant who has breast implants.
Example

Participant criteria:

  • 2 participants with access needs
  • ideally from a range of socio-economic backgrounds
  • ideally with a mix of technical confidence
  • must be aged 18+ (mix of age ranges if possible)
  • must have access to their own iOS (Apple) or Android smartphone
  • must have used an NHS service (such as a GP, hospital, pharmacy, dentist, optician) in the last 12 months
  • must not have worked for the NHS or in healthcare in the past 5 years
  • must not have worked in digital, IT or technology professions in the past 5 years
  • must not have participated in any NHS research in the last 12 months
